Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India
15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015

Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam was one of India’s most respected scientists and the 11th President of India. He is widely known as the "Missile Man of India" for his contributions to the country’s space and defense programs.

He played a major role in developing India’s missile technology and was also a key figure in the nuclear tests conducted by India. His work made India stronger in science and technology.

Apart from being a great scientist, he was also an inspiring teacher. He motivated millions of students to dream big and work hard. His vision was to see India as a developed nation.

Dr. Kalam was known for his simplicity, humility, and dedication to education. His life continues to inspire people all over the world.
